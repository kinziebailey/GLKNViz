The "Correlations" graph plots two parameters against each other. Each point on the graph is a pair of measurements taken on the same date at the same location. The "Correlations" graph shows the mean of the site visit's measurements when more than one measurement was taken for a parameter on one site visit.

Correlation Table

Parameter 1 Parameter 2 Correlation Type Description
Air Temperature Water Temperature Positive Higher air temperatures raise water temperature.
Conductivity SPC / TDS / Salinity Positive All reflect dissolved ion concentrations.
Conductivity Total N / Total P Positive Nutrient loading increases dissolved ion content.
Cross Section Depth Wetted Width Positive Wider streams are often deeper in natural systems.
Discharge ANC (Alkalinity) Variable Higher discharge can increase or decrease ANC.
Discharge Cross Section Depth / Wetted Width Positive Greater flow expands stream cross-section.
Discharge Velocity Positive Higher discharge usually increases flow speed.
Discharge DO Concentration Variable Higher discharge can increase DO via aeration or dilute it depending on conditions.
pH ANC (Alkalinity) Positive ANC buffers pH; higher ANC stabilizes or raises pH.
Salinity TDS / Conductivity / SPC Positive All are measures of dissolved ions.
SPC (Specific Conductance) TDS / Salinity / Conductivity Positive SPC is temperature-corrected conductivity; all are highly correlated.
TDS Conductivity / SPC / Salinity Positive More dissolved ions raise TDS and conductivity.
Total N Total P Positive Common sources (agriculture, sewage) raise both nutrients.
Total N or P DO Concentration Negative (indirect) Nutrients promote eutrophication, which may lead to oxygen depletion.
Velocity DO Concentration Positive (general) Higher velocity enhances mixing and oxygenation.
Water Temperature DO Concentration Negative Warmer water holds less oxygen.
Water Temperature DO Saturation Negative Saturation decreases with higher temperature.
Water Temperature pH Negative (general) pH can decline with increasing temperature due to chemical equilibria.
Water Temperature Conductivity / SPC Positive (slight) Ion mobility increases with temperature, slightly increasing conductivity.
Water Temperature TDS Neutral TDS itself doesn't change, but its conductivity response does.

Introduction

The Great Lakes Inventory and Monitoring Network (GLKN) is part of the National Park Service (NPS) Inventory and Monitoring Division. GLKN conducts long-term ecological monitoring in the following parks: Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, Grand Portage National Monument, Indiana Dunes National Park, Isle Royale National Park, Mississippi National River and Recreation Area, Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore, Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, St. Croix National Scenic Riverway, and Voyageurs National Park. GLKN’s inventory and monitoring work provides park managers with status and trends data on vital signs that represent the health of natural resources, including lake, river, and stream water quality, in most parks in the GLKN.

Instructions

This visualizer tool allows users to explore and easily download water quality data. It uses data published in the
USGS Water Quality Portal (WQP). The WQP integrates and provides access to publicly available water-quality data from databases such as USGS NWIS and BioData, EPA STORET, and USDA-ARS STEWARDS through a single search interface. More information on GLKN’s water quality data and constituent databases is in the About tab.

This visualizer plots data collected by the GLKN. To run the visualizer, follow these basic steps below:

  1. Select Park of interest
  2. Select one or more Site of interest
  3. The map will zoom to the last Site selected
  4. Select the Plot of interest from the tabs at the top
  5. Select a Parameter(s) to plot
  6. Select Years of interest

General Notes

  1. All data in this visualizer are fully QAQC'd and available to the public.
  2. Data are added to this visualizer annually, with an approximately one-year lag from the current year to allow for full data QAQC and publication.
  3. To be included in this visualizer, a site must have at least five years of data available or be actively monitored by GLKN.
  4. Not all parameters are available for every site. For example, at our river sites we measure water clarity with total suspended solids and not with a Secchi disk, whereas at our lake sites the opposite is true.
  5. Secchi disk depth, bottom depth, and water level are physical measurements not associated with collection of a water sample.
  6. Water samples are collected near the surface in the upper 2 meters of the lake, river, or stream.
  7. The Profile Plots tab only shows water quality parameters measured with a sonde from the surface to the bottom of a water body.
  8. The Time Series, Boxplot, and Correlation Plot tabbs only show data collected in the upper 2 meters, except for parameters collected as physical measurements.

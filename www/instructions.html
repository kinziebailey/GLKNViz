Introduction

The Great Lakes Region Inventory and Monitoring Network (GLKN) is part of the National Parks Service (NPS) Inventory and Monitoring Division. GLKN conducts long-term ecological monitoring in the following parks: Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, Grand Portage National Monument, Indiana Dunes National Lakeshore, Isle Royale National Park, Mississippi National river and Recreation Area, Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore, Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, St. Croix National scenic Riverway, and Voyageurs National Park. This program is designed to provide park managers with status and trends data on vital signs that represent the health of natural resources in the above stated parks.

Instructions

This visualizer tool allows users to explore and analyze water quality data. It uses data published on the
USGS Water Quality Portal.

This visualizer plots data collected by the GLKN. To run the visulizer, follow the steps below:

  1. Select Park of interest
  2. Select one or more Site of interest
  3. Select a Parameter to plot
  4. Select Years of interest

Each plot has a Graphics Options, an Abouth this Graph, and plot controls. The Graphics Options allow you to control the figure size, text and points sizes, line width, and threshold line color. The Abouth this Graph contains information about each figure created in the visualizer.


This visualizer tool operates using R Shiny code. A static copy of the application source code is available on NPS IRMA Data Store:
DataStore - National Capital Region Network Water Visualizer (R Shiny application)

The development version of the source code is available at:
https://github.com/NCRN/NCRNWaterViz/tree/ncrn_refactor

Parameters

GLKN monitors the water quality of inland lakes in five parks: Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, Indiana Dunes National Park, Isle Royale National Park, Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore, Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, and Voyageurs National Park. The montiroing protocol is available on NPS IRMA Data Store (GLKN Inland Lakes WQ Monitoring Protocol). The key variables of interest measured for lakes are:

  1. Temperature
  2. Specific Electrical Conductivity (EC25 or SC25)
  3. pH
  4. Dissolved Oxygen (Concentration and % Saturation)
  5. Lake Level
  6. Water Clarity
  7. Major Ions
  8. Dissolved Silica (SiO2)
  9. Dissolved Organic Carbon (DOC)
  10. Nutrients
  11. Chlorophyll-a

GLKN additionally mesures water quality for large rivers at St. Croix National Scenic Riverway. The montiroing protocol is available on NPS IRMA Data Store (GLKN Large River WQ Monitoring Protocol). The key variables of interest measured for rivers are:

  1. Temperature
  2. Specific Electrical Conductivity (EC25 or SC25)
  3. pH
  4. Dissolved Oxygen (Concentration and % Saturation)
  5. Flow
  6. Turbidity
  7. Nutrients
  8. Chlorophyll-a
  9. Major Ions
  10. Dissolved Silica (SiO2)
  11. Dissolved Organic Carbon (DOC)
  12. Water Clarity
